End of the cyber
warfare

What is the cyber world?

What is
cyberwar?

Cyberwarfare is the use of computers and the Internet to wage war in cyberspace. Cyberspace" is a universe of information, digitised megadata, a means of communication.

Roughly speaking, it is an immaterial universe that cannot be touched. It is located throughout the world's computers.
Inside it is all the information posted on the internet since its creation.

How do we wage war in cyberspace?

he cyberwar is therefore fought within this space. There are several types of crime on the Internet.

The main one is the theft of data through schemes.
The installation of malware on your device, through emails, files.

In 2017, more than 700 million cyber attacks were detected worldwide.
For damages of 550 billion euros, including 6 in France.

This war is now taken seriously, countries are spending a lot to defend themselves.
Edward Snowden is a former employee of the CIA and the NSA.

He revealed that a lot of countries spy on their own population and those of other countries.

He revealed programs like "Tempora", a British program.

"Prism", a programme involving Yahoo, Google, Facebook, Youtube, Microsoft, Skype, and Apple.
All these companies have given the NSA access to their servers.

France developed "Babar", a spyware developed by the DGSE.
Barbar's main target is Iran and African countries.

Why is cyberspace not used properly?

The use of cyberspace is disappointing.

With all the knowledge available on the Internet, we can find new techniques.
To find a man with a superior intelligence to the rest of the population.
Like Albert Einstein or Sun Tzu in their time.

We might already have made a device to walk on water.
Or create equipment to fly, even if it is pretentious.

In short, the internet could be put to better use.

I fear that this war will never end.
And if it does end, where will it lead us?
